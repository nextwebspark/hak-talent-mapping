An AI-powered operating system that replaces the entire executive search process — from strategic brief to final recommendation — with intelligence, speed, and defensible evidence.

## 01 — The Problem

**Executive search is broken. Expensive, slow, and opaque.**

Companies spend hundreds of thousands of dollars on executive search firms that take weeks to deliver a shortlist — with no transparency into how they got there, and no guarantee of quality.

### Problem 01 — The Research Problem
Building a target company universe takes senior researchers 4–8 hours per search. Adjacent sectors — often the richest source of talent — are systematically missed. Lists built in spreadsheets have no rationale, no confidence scoring, and cannot be refined intelligently.

### Problem 02 — The Opacity Problem
Search firms present a shortlist after 8–12 weeks and say "trust us." Clients have no visibility into how candidates were found, why they were selected, or what the broader talent pool looks like. Assessment is qualitative, subjective, and undefendable.

### Problem 03 — The Cost Problem
Executive search fees run 20–30% of first-year salary — $50,000 to $500,000+ per placement. Mid-level recruitment costs $15,000–$30,000. These fees are paid for a process that is largely manual research, pattern matching, and network access — all of which AI can now do better.

The search firm model was built for a world before AI. They charge for access to information, networks, and research capability that any well-designed system can now deliver faster, more accurately, and at a fraction of the cost.

## 02 — The Solution

**A five-layer AI operating system for the entire search lifecycle.**

Every layer of the executive search process — from brief to hire — handled by AI with more intelligence, more speed, and more transparency than any human team.

### Layer 01 — Strategic

**What it does:** Ingests briefing notes, position descriptions, and company data to build a deep understanding of the client, the role, the industry landscape, and the talent market. Constructs a weighted search mandate — ranking role requirements by criticality, fingerprinting company culture, and defining the total addressable talent universe across direct and adjacent sectors. Client validates and locks the mandate before execution begins.

**What it replaces:** The senior search partner intake meeting, market briefing, and search strategy document. Typically 2–3 weeks and $15,000+ of consultant time.

---

### Layer 02 — Execution

**What it does:** Builds a brief-specific company universe across direct and adjacent sectors — not a generic filter, but an intelligently constructed list shaped by the strategic mandate. Clients filter and select companies, sharpening the addressable market. The system then searches across people data providers to surface executives at target and adjacent seniority levels within those validated companies. Every candidate is scoped to the agreed mandate.

**What it replaces:** The research phase. Weeks of desk research, database searches, and company mapping. Typically 3–4 weeks of researcher time across internal and outsourced teams.

---

### Layer 03 — Outreach

**What it does:** AI drafts bespoke outreach messages for each candidate — referencing their specific background, career trajectory, and why this role is relevant to them. Not templated, not automated spray. The human reviews, refines if needed, and sends — owning the relationship from first contact. Responses are tracked, sentiment analysed, and follow-up timing recommended. Full pipeline management built in.

**What it replaces:** The outreach and candidate engagement phase. Search consultants spend 30–40% of their time on personalised candidate approach and relationship management.

---

### Layer 04 — Analysis

**What it does:** Ingests all candidate data — CVs, email responses, press coverage, screening notes — and produces a structured assessment across four dimensions:
- **Technical Fit** — scored against weighted position description criteria
- **Cultural Fit** — scored against company fingerprint
- **Personality Fit** — inferred from all data
- **Leadership Capability** — assessed for senior roles

Every score has evidence, a confidence level, and a defensible rationale. Profiles update dynamically as new data arrives — across searches, across time.

**What it replaces:** The screening and assessment phase. Typically 2–4 weeks of calls, interviews, and subjective evaluation by consultants whose methodology is opaque and unverifiable.

---

### Layer 05 — Recommendation

**What it does:** A Search Command Centre that gives clients total clarity:
- Live dashboard showing pipeline progress
- 9-box talent map plotting all candidates by Brief Fit vs. Readiness
- Ranked shortlist with full evidence breakdown
- AI recommendation engine surfacing top candidates with contextualised rationale — not just the highest score, but the right strategic choice for the client's specific situation and risk appetite

**What it replaces:** The final shortlist presentation and recommendation — the moment search firms charge the most for and justify their entire fee. Delivered in real time, not after 8 weeks.

## 05 — Competitive Moat

**Why this cannot be easily replicated.**

The platform's defensibility compounds over time. Every search makes it smarter. Every client makes it more valuable.

### The Living Intelligence Database
Every candidate profile enriches over time and across searches. By Search 10, you have assessments that would take a search firm years of relationships to build. This proprietary executive intelligence database is an asset no competitor can acquire — it must be earned through use.

### The Connected Pipeline
Competitors own one layer. This platform owns five — and the weighted brief travels through all of them. The strategic mandate shapes execution, outreach, analysis, and recommendation. That coherence is structurally impossible to replicate with point solutions bolted together.

### Network Effects
More clients → more searches → more candidate data → smarter profiles → better results → more clients. The platform becomes measurably more accurate and valuable with every search run. A new entrant starting today would need years of data accumulation to reach parity.

### Switching Cost
Once a company has run 10 searches — their mandates, preferred company universes, assessed candidates, and hiring patterns are institutionalised in the platform. Leaving means starting over. The longer they use it, the more it knows about their specific hiring context.
