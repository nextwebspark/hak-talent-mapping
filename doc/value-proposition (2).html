01 — The Problem

Executive search is broken.
Expensive, slow, and opaque.

Companies spend hundreds of thousands of dollars on executive search firms that take weeks to deliver a shortlist — with no transparency into how they got there, and no guarantee of quality.

01
The Research Problem
Building a target company universe takes senior researchers 4–8 hours per search. Adjacent sectors — often the richest source of talent — are systematically missed. Lists built in spreadsheets have no rationale, no confidence scoring, and cannot be refined intelligently.
02
The Opacity Problem
Search firms present a shortlist after 8–12 weeks and say "trust us." Clients have no visibility into how candidates were found, why they were selected, or what the broader talent pool looks like. Assessment is qualitative, subjective, and undefendable.
03
The Cost Problem
Executive search fees run 20–30% of first-year salary — $50,000 to $500,000+ per placement. Mid-level recruitment costs $15,000–$30,000. These fees are paid for a process that is largely manual research, pattern matching, and network access — all of which AI can now do better.

The search firm model was built for a world before AI. They charge for access to information, networks, and research capability that any well-designed system can now deliver faster, more accurately, and at a fraction of the cost.

02 — The Solution

A five-layer AI operating system
for the entire search lifecycle.

Every layer of the executive search process — from brief to hire — handled by AI with more intelligence, more speed, and more transparency than any human team.

Layer 01
Strategic
What it does

Ingests briefing notes, position descriptions, and company data to build a deep understanding of the client, the role, the industry landscape, and the talent market. Constructs a weighted search mandate — ranking role requirements by criticality, fingerprinting company culture, and defining the total addressable talent universe across direct and adjacent sectors. Client validates and locks the mandate before execution begins.

What it replaces

The senior search partner intake meeting, market briefing, and search strategy document. Typically 2–3 weeks and $15,000+ of consultant time.

Layer 02
Execution
What it does

Builds a brief-specific company universe across direct and adjacent sectors — not a generic filter, but an intelligently constructed list shaped by the strategic mandate. Clients filter and select companies, sharpening the addressable market. The system then searches across people data providers to surface executives at target and adjacent seniority levels within those validated companies. Every candidate is scoped to the agreed mandate.

What it replaces

The research phase. Weeks of desk research, database searches, and company mapping. Typically 3–4 weeks of researcher time across internal and outsourced teams.

Layer 03
Outreach
What it does

AI drafts bespoke outreach messages for each candidate — referencing their specific background, career trajectory, and why this role is relevant to them. Not templated, not automated spray. The human reviews, refines if needed, and sends — owning the relationship from first contact. Responses are tracked, sentiment analysed, and follow-up timing recommended. Full pipeline management built in.

What it replaces

The outreach and candidate engagement phase. Search consultants spend 30–40% of their time on personalised candidate approach and relationship management.

Layer 04
Analysis
What it does

Ingests all candidate data — CVs, email responses, press coverage, screening notes — and produces a structured assessment across four dimensions: Technical Fit (scored against weighted PD criteria), Cultural Fit (against company fingerprint), Personality Fit (inferred from all data), and Leadership Capability (for senior roles). Every score has evidence, a confidence level, and a defensible rationale. Profiles update dynamically as new data arrives — across searches, across time.

What it replaces

The screening and assessment phase. Typically 2–4 weeks of calls, interviews, and subjective evaluation by consultants whose methodology is opaque and unverifiable.

Layer 05
Recommendation
What it does

A Search Command Centre that gives clients total clarity: a live dashboard showing pipeline progress, a 9-box talent map plotting all candidates by Brief Fit vs. Readiness, ranked shortlist with full evidence breakdown, and an AI recommendation engine that surfaces the top candidates with contextualised rationale — not just the highest score, but the right strategic choice for this client's specific situation and risk appetite.

What it replaces

The final shortlist presentation and recommendation — the moment search firms charge the most for and justify their entire fee. Delivered in real time, not after 8 weeks.

"Where search firms say trust us after 8 weeks — we say here's the evidence in real time."
